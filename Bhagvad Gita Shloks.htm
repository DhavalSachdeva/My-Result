BHAGWAT GEETA SHLOKS AND MEANINGS.

 

चातुर्वर्ण्यं मया सृष्टं गुणकर्मविभागश: |
तस्य कर्तारमपि मां विद्ध्यकर्तारमव्ययम् || 4.13||

Meaning- The four categories of occupations were created by me according to people’s qualities and activities. Although I am the creator of this system, know me to be the non-doer and eternal.

 

योगिनामपि सर्वेषां मद्गतेनान्तरात्मना |
श्रद्धावान्भजते यो मां स मे युक्ततमो मत: || 6.47||

Meaning- Of all yogis, those whose minds are always absorbed in me, and who engage in devotion to me with great faith, them I consider to be the highest of all.

 

भोक्तारं यज्ञतपसां सर्वलोकमहेश्वरम् |
सुहृदं सर्वभूतानां ज्ञात्वा मां शान्तिमृच्छति || 5.29|

Meaning- Having realized Me as the enjoyer of all sacrifices and austerities, the Supreme Lord of all the worlds and the selfless Friend of all living beings, my devotee attains peace.

 

न मां दुष्कृतिनो मूढा: प्रपद्यन्ते नराधमा: |
माययापहृतज्ञाना आसुरं भावमाश्रिता: || 7.15||

Meaning- In this verse, Shree Krishna says that there are four categories of people who do not surrender to Him:

1. The Ignorant. People who lack spiritual knowledge and are not aware of the process of surrendering to God.

2. The lazy. These are the ones who have the knowledge and awareness of what they need to do still, due to their lazy-nature do not want to take any initiative to surrender.

3. Misled / Tricked Intellects. People who are so proud of their intellects that they have no faith in the scriptures. Since they do not have faith, they are unable to practice surrender to God.

4. Demonic Nature. These are the people who are aware of God and His purpose for the world but still work against Him. Surrender to God is not at all expected from them.

 

मयाध्यक्षेण प्रकृति: सूयते सचराचरम् |
हेतुनानेन कौन्तेय जगद्विपरिवर्तते || 9.10||

Meaning- God does not directly engage in the work of creating life forms.  God has various energies and souls appointed by Him for the purpose to do it under His command.  For example, the President of a country does not personally do every task of the government.  He has various departments under Him, and officials appointed for performing the different functions.  And yet, the accomplishments and failures of the government are attributed to Him.

 

पत्रं पुष्पं फलं तोयं यो मे भक्त्या प्रयच्छति |
तदहं भक्त्युपहृतमश्नामि प्रयतात्मन: || 9.26||

Meaning- If one offers to Me with devotion a leaf, a flower, a fruit, or even water, I delightfully partake of that article offered with love by My devotee in pure consciousness.

 

यत्करोषि यदश्नासि यज्जुहोषि ददासि यत् |
यत्तपस्यसि कौन्तेय तत्कुरुष्व मदर्पणम् || 9.27||

Meaning- God says that all actions should be offered to Him.  Whatever social duties you perform, vegetarian food you eat, non-alcoholic beverages you drink, Vedic rites you perform should all be offered mentally to the Supreme Lord.  Very often, people separate devotion from their daily life, and look on it as something that is only to be performed inside the temple room.  However, devotion is not to be restricted to the periphery of the temple room; it is to be engaged in at every moment of our life. 

Sage Narad defines bhakti in this manner:

“Devotion means offering your every activity to God, and feeling intense separation if ever you lose remembrance of Him.” 

 

ये तु सर्वाणि कर्माणि मयि संन्न्यस्य मत्पर: |
अनन्येनैव योगेन मां ध्यायन्त उपासते || 12.6||

Meaning- Shree Krishna says to Arjun, those who dedicate all their actions to Me, regarding Me as the Supreme goal, worshiping Me and meditating on Me with exclusive devotion, they easily attain me and are no more revolved in the ocean of life and death.

 

तेषामहं समुद्धर्ता मृत्युसंसारसागरात् |
भवामि नचिरात्पार्थ मय्यावेशितचेतसाम् || 12.7||

Meaning- Shree Krishna says to Arjun, those who dedicate all their actions to Me, regarding Me as the Supreme goal, worshiping Me and meditating on Me with exclusive devotion, they easily attain me and are no more revolved in the ocean of life and death.

 

क्षिप्रं भवति धर्मात्मा शश्वच्छान्तिं निगच्छति |
कौन्तेय प्रतिजानीहि न मे भक्त: प्रणश्यति || 9.31||

Meaning- In this verse Shree Krishna explains that if devotees continue the process of exclusive devotion with steady faith in God, their hearts will become purified and they will swiftly develop saintly qualities. Shree Krishna asks Arjun to boldly declare that His devotees will never perish.  He does not say, “The jñānī (person of knowledge) will not be lost.”  Nor does He say, “The karmī (performer of rituals) shall not perish.”  He makes His promise for His bhaktas (devotees), saying that “they shall never come to loss.”

 

सर्वधर्मान्परित्यज्य मामेकं शरणं व्रज |
अहं त्वां सर्वपापेभ्यो मोक्षयिष्यामि मा शुच: || 18.66||

Meaning- All along, Shree Krishna had been asking Arjun to do two things simultaneously—engage his mind in devotion, and engage his body in fulfilling his material duty as a warrior. Now, Shree Krishna reverses this teaching by saying that here is no need to fulfil even material dharma. Arjun can renounce all material duties and simply surrender to God. This is the principle of karm sanyās. Shree Krishna tells Arjun not to fear; he will pardon him from all sins, and free him from material existence.

 

 

Overall Summary: -

In these verses God says, “The four categories of occupations were created by me according to people’s qualities and activities.”. These are four categories of people who do not surrender to Him:

1.     The Ignorant. People who lack spiritual knowledge.

 

2.   The lazy. These are the ones who due to their lazy-nature do not want to take any initiative to surrender.

 

3.   Misled / Tricked Intellects. Since they do not have faith, they are unable to practice surrender to God.

 

4. Demonic Nature. Surrender to God is not at all expected from these people.

Although I am the creator of this system, know me to be the non-doer and eternal.

God does not directly engage in the work of creating life forms.  God has various energies and souls appointed by Him for the purpose to do it under His command.

 

God says that all actions should be offered to Him.

Whatever offered to Me with devotion, I delightfully partake of that article offered with love by My devotee in pure consciousness.

 

Shree Krishna says to Arjun, those who dedicate all their actions to Me, regarding Me as the Supreme goal, worshiping Me and meditating on Me with exclusive devotion, they easily attain me and attain peace.
